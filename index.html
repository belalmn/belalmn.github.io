Experience
Data Science Intern @ Qatar Islamic Bank (Summer 2024)
During my internship at Qatar Islamic Bank's Data Science Division, I developed an ETL pipeline to parse and analyze emails stored in .pst files. This pipeline extracted, transformed, and structured email data to provide actionable insights for the bank's operations department.
One of the key focuses of this project was to explore the integration of modern AI techniques for handling unstructured data. I utilized Open Source Large Language Models (LLMs), including Llama3.1, Phi3-Mini, alongside traditional methods to classify emails, extract key features, and identify patterns relevant to business operations.
Additionally, I conducted in-depth evaluations to compare the effectiveness of LLM-based methods versus conventional techniques. I wrote and presented various technical documents with key indicators for why certian models or approaches worked for the business needs, and providing insights into the practical adoption of modern LLM based tools in this financial institution.
This internship allowed me to apply advanced data science and machine learning concepts in a real-world setting, strengthening my expertise in ETL processes, data analysis, and leveraging AI for enterprise solutions. You can find more details on the project here.
Software Engineering Intern @ Qatar Science & Technology Park (Summer 2024)
I interned at Qatar Science & Technology Park, a startup hub, and worked directly with GPTBots, a no-code platform that allows enterprise clients to configure their own agentic AI "bots" to serve a variety of purposes, ranging from customer support to SEO Writing to more active roles, such as LLM driven Data Analysis.
My task during this internship was to explore Multi-Agent based systems, particularly Microsoft Autogen, as a way to strengthen the quality of the bots and improve the user experience.
I developed a proof of concept using Microsoft Autogen and LangChain, by creating an SEO Writing bot that could generate content for a variety of topics. This involved creating a pipeline of multiple agents with their own specifications and configurations, and integrating them into a single system that could generate content based on user input. I also worked on improving the quality of LLM output through prompt engineering, using techniques like Chain-of-Thought and Few-Shot Prompting.
Overall my exploration of Multi-Agent systems and my feedback on the capabilities of Autogen and LangChain has helped pivot the direction of the company towards a more robust and user-friendly platform.
